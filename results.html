üî¨ Research Question Findings

RQ1

How frequently do hybrid-app-specific issues appear in the development histories?

Our analysis of 589 issues across hybrid Android repositories revealed that 54 issues (9.2%) are directly related to hybrid-specific challenges. Similarly, out of 1,933 commits analyzed, 13 commits (0.7%) contained hybrid-related changes. This indicates that while hybrid issues represent a minority of overall development activity, they constitute a significant and identifiable category of software challenges.

RQ2

What categories of hybrid-specific issues are most commonly reported?

The most prevalent category is WebView Configuration (20 issues, 37%), followed by Cross-Language Communication (14 issues, 26%) and Performance Issues (11 issues, 20%). Security and lifecycle issues are less common but still present in the dataset.

RQ3

How do hybrid-specific issues manifest in development artifacts?

Hybrid issues primarily manifest through crash reports and error messages containing WebView-related exceptions. The most common patterns include WebView crashes, JavaScript callback failures, and cross-language communication errors. The top keywords detected in issues are shown below:

Top Keywords in Issues:
webview.*crash error.*webview javascript.*callback native.*web callback loading.*slow bridge memory JavascriptInterface WebSettings
RQ4

How do these issues differ across sensitive domains?

From GitHub repositories, we found 22 education-related, 11 finance-related, 10 public services, and 2 healthcare repositories in sensitive domains. The distribution suggests that education apps are more commonly developed as hybrid applications, while healthcare apps may favor native development for reliability and compliance reasons.
